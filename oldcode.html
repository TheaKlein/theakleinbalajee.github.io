ClearFlow — Analytics Redesign

A full redesign of a B2B analytics platform, restructuring complex enterprise workflows to reduce friction and surface the right data at the right moment.

RoleLead UX Designer
Duration14 weeks
ToolsFigma · Maze · FigJam · Notion
Team2 designers · 3 engineers

The Problem

ClearFlow's analytics platform had grown organically over 4 years, accumulating features without coherent structure. Power users were completing critical tasks through 5–7 step workarounds. New users couldn't orient themselves within the first session. Churn data pointed clearly to onboarding and daily workflow friction as the key drivers.

Research & Findings

I conducted 18 contextual inquiry interviews across 6 enterprise clients, ran a competitive audit across 8 analytics tools, and facilitated 3 card-sorting sessions to establish a new IA baseline.

Task completion
57%
Of users completed key tasks on first attempt in the original design — below the industry baseline of 78%.
Time-on-task
4.2×
Longer than benchmark for primary workflows, driven by navigation and search failures.
Nav confusion rate
71%
Of participants expressed confusion about primary navigation during moderated testing sessions.
Feature awareness
38%
Of core features were unknown to active users who would benefit from them.

Design Approach

The redesign focused on three principles: surface-first navigation (most-used tools always visible), progressive disclosure for complex features, and a unified data model that made relationships between metrics legible.

Outcomes

Task completion 57% → 91%
Time-on-task −38%
NPS score +24 pts
Feature discovery +55%
Churn indicator −12% at 6mo

Shipped to production in Q3 2024 with a phased rollout across 200+ enterprise accounts. Design system handed off and adopted by engineering team for all subsequent feature work.
